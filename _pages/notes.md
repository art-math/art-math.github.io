These are some expository notes I've written for classes, talks, etc. For the most part they do not contain original material.

<a href="/files/tangentspace.pdf">pdf</a>&nbsp;&nbsp;<a href="#" onclick="toggle_visibility('firstnotes'); return false;">abs</a>&nbsp;&nbsp; Equivalent definitions of the tangent space
<div id="firstnotes" style='display:none; margin-left: 1em;'>
<b>Abstract:</b>
We prove the equivalence of several common definitions of the tangent space to a smooth manifold, and then show that this equivalence is "natural" in the sense that the differentials are related via factorization through the corresponding isomorphism between any two tangent spaces. Notes for MATH 518: Differentiable Manifolds I taken with Susan Tolman in Spring 2021.
</div>

<a href="/files/liegroups.pdf">pdf</a>&nbsp;&nbsp;<a href="#" onclick="toggle_visibility('secondnotes'); return false;">abs</a>&nbsp;&nbsp; Lie groups and quotient manifolds
<div id="secondnotes" style='display:none; margin-left: 1em;'>
<b>Abstract:</b>
We present all of the necessary tools and techniques to state the quotient manifold theorem: given a smooth, proper, free Lie group action on a smooth manifold, the orbit space is a smooth manifold. Then we explore some important applications of the theorem, such as the construction and characterization of homogeneous spaces. Notes for MATH 518: Differentiable Manifolds I taken with Susan Tolman in Spring 2021.
</div>

<a href="/files/idealtetrahedra.pdf">pdf</a>&nbsp;&nbsp;<a href="#" onclick="toggle_visibility('thirdnotes'); return false;">abs</a>&nbsp;&nbsp; Shape parameters of ideal tetrahedra
<div id="thirdnotes" style='display:none; margin-left: 1em;'>
<b>Abstract:</b>
We define the notion of ideal tetrahedron and the shape parameters associated with its edges in hyperbolic space and prove some basic facts about them. Final report for a topics course taken with Nathan Dunfield in Fall 2021. 
</div>
